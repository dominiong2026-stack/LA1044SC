Freemasonry

A disciplined fraternity for moral reflection, fellowship, charity, and service.

Freemasonry uses allegory, symbolism, ceremony, and fellowship to encourage self-improvement, integrity, benevolence, and duty to others.

The Antient Charge

Duty, attendance, and obligation.

From Antient times no Master or Fellow could be absent from his Lodge, especially when warned to appear at it, without incurring censure, unless pure necessity hindered him.

Brotherly LoveRespect, civility, fraternity, and mutual support among Brethren.
ReliefCharitable duty, care, benevolence, and practical concern for others.
TruthIntegrity, moral seriousness, reflection, and personal discipline.

Scottish Constitution

Lodge Alexandra works under the Grand Lodge of Scotland and within the District Grand Lodge of Trinidad and Tobago and Grenada.

Formation

The Lodge encourages discipline, self-command, discretion, and seriousness in private and public life.

Fellowship

Brethren meet in a structured environment that values respect, trust, and continuity.

Masonic charity

The Lodge recognises charity as a practical expression of Masonic duty. TTMPS and TTMCO are identified as part of the local Masonic charitable landscape; full descriptions can be added once final approved wording is supplied.
